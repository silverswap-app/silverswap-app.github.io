SilverSwap AG token and Sonic DEX

SilverSwap is a Sonic-network decentralized exchange built around swaps, concentrated liquidity, farms, auctions, and the AG token. The official site positions SilverSwap as a high-performance DEX for low-fee trading on Sonic, while its documentation describes AG as the token used across tokenomics, auctions, burns, and liquidity incentives. Searchers looking for SilverSwap usually want to know whether it is a real Sonic DEX, what AG does, and where activity happens. The practical answer is that swaps and liquidity are handled on silverswap.io, while token details, contract addresses, audits, and community links live in the SilverSwap GitBook.

SilverSwap fees, Snatch and Flare auctions

SilverSwap's most brand-specific feature is its auction system. The docs describe 12-hour auction cycles where users bid AG, with Snatch tied to fees from individual liquidity pools and Flare tied to a broader buyback action chosen by the winning participant. The official site says auction mechanics route a large share of DEX fees toward auctions, while documentation separates Snatch, Flare, and burn mechanics. For SEO intent, this matters because SilverSwap is not just a basic swap interface; searchers comparing it with other Sonic DEXs are often looking for how AG, fees, burns, auctions, and liquidity participation connect.

SilverSwap concentrated liquidity pools

SilverSwap supports concentrated liquidity, meaning liquidity providers can place capital inside selected price ranges instead of passively covering every price. That can improve capital efficiency when a pair trades inside the chosen range, but it also requires more active management because liquidity outside the current price range stops earning swap fees. SilverSwap docs include guides for adding liquidity to existing pools and adding initial liquidity for advanced users. For searchers asking about SilverSwap APR, the accurate answer is that LP returns are variable and depend on pair volume, fee tier, price range, incentives, and impermanent loss.

SilverSwap audit and contract addresses

SilverSwap publishes smart contract links and audit references in its documentation. The smart contract address page links core swap and liquidity contracts such as factory, vault, router, quoter, NFT position manager, farming contracts, and subgraphs on Sonic infrastructure. The audit page states that on-chain contract audits are performed by Hashlock and links to the audit details. Users should verify contract addresses from the official docs before approving tokens, because Sonic DEX lookalikes and copied token tickers can appear quickly after launch. Contract verification is more important than relying on social links alone.

How to bridge funds to SilverSwap on Sonic

SilverSwap itself is a DEX on Sonic, so users need assets on the Sonic network before swapping or providing liquidity. Its FAQ points users toward bridge providers such as Stargate, Squid Router, or Wormhole Portal for moving funds from BSC or other chains into Sonic. Once funds arrive, users still need Sonic gas for transactions and should confirm the target token contract before swapping. The search intent here is practical: SilverSwap is not an all-chain exchange; it is a Sonic venue, so bridging, wallet network selection, gas, slippage, and approval checks are part of the real workflow.

SilverSwap FAQ

What is SilverSwap?

SilverSwap is a decentralized exchange on the Sonic network. It supports token swaps, liquidity provision, farming, and an auction-driven tokenomics system around AG. The project describes itself as a premium DEX, with official documentation covering concentrated liquidity, farms, audits, smart contract addresses, and community channels.

What is the AG token on SilverSwap?

AG is the token used in the SilverSwap ecosystem. The official site describes AG as foundational to buybacks, liquidity provider rewards, auctions, and fee distribution. The docs also describe burn mechanisms and 12-hour auctions where participants bid AG. Token price and supply data should be checked through market listings and official contract references.

When did SilverSwap AG launch?

SilverSwap's FAQ states that AG launched on December 18, 2024, and farms launched on December 24, 2024. That date is useful for searchers checking whether AG is a new Sonic token or an older DEX asset. Always pair launch-date information with contract verification before buying or approving any token.

Is SilverSwap audited?

SilverSwap documentation says its on-chain contract audits are done by Hashlock and links to audit details. An audit does not guarantee that a protocol is risk-free. Users still need to consider smart contract risk, liquidity risk, token volatility, oracle or routing assumptions, and the possibility of approving malicious lookalike contracts.

What network does SilverSwap use?

SilverSwap runs on Sonic. Users need a wallet connected to Sonic and enough native gas for approvals, swaps, and liquidity actions. Assets from Ethereum, BNB Chain, or other networks must be bridged to Sonic through a supported bridge provider before they can be used in SilverSwap pools.

Where can I buy AG?

The SilverSwap FAQ links users to the SilverSwap app for buying AG. CoinGecko also lists AG under the Silver token page, with Sonic ecosystem metadata. Before buying, users should verify the token contract from official SilverSwap documentation or a reputable market listing and avoid relying on ticker symbols alone.
